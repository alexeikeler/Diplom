The Ones Who Walk Away (уходи) From Omelas   

With a clamor of bells that set the swallows soaring,(ласточки парящие)  the Festival of Summer came (пришло лето) to the city Omelas,(город омелас)  bright-towered by the sea.

The ringing of the boats in harbor sparkled (гавань сверкала) with flags.

In the streets between houses with red roofs (красные крыши) and painted walls,(окрашенные стены)  between old moss-(старый мох) grown  gardens and under avenues of trees, past great parks (мимо великих парков) and public buildings,(общественные здания)  processions moved.(процессии двинулись)   

Some were decorous: old people (старые люди) in long stiff robes (длинные жесткие халаты) of mauve and gray, grave master workmen,(могильные мастера)  quiet, merry women carrying (веселые женщины, несущие) their babies and chatting as they walked.

In other streets the music beat faster,(музыка бьется быстрее)  a shimmering of gong and tambourine, and the people went dancing,(люди пошли танцевать)  the procession was a dance.

Children dodged (дети увернулись) in and out, their high calls rising like (высокие звонки растут, как) the swallows' crossing flights (пересечение рейсов) over the music and the singing.

  

All the processions wound towards (процессии свернулись в сторону) the north side (Северная сторона) of the city, where on the great water-(отличная вода) meadow called (луг называется) the Green Fields boys (мальчики зеленые поля) and girls, naked in the bright air,(яркий воздух)  with mud-stained feet (испачканные ноги) and ankles and long, lithe arms,(гибкие руки) exercised their restive horses (норовистые лошади) before the race.   

The horses wore (лошади носили) no gear at all but a halter without bit.(недоуздок без удил) 

Their manes were braided with streamers of silver, gold, and green.

They flared their nostrils and pranced and boasted to one another;(друг друга)  they were vastly excited,(чрезвычайно взволнован)  the horse being the only animal who has adopted our ceremonies as his own.   

Far off to the north and west the mountains stood (горы стояли) up half encircling Omelas (полуокружающий омелас) on her bay.

The air of morning was so clear that the snow still crowning (снег еще венчает) the Eighteen Peaks burned (восемнадцать пиков сожжены) with white-gold fire across (золотой огонь) the miles of sunlit air,(солнечный воздух)  under the dark blue (темно-синий) of the sky.

There was just enough wind (достаточно ветра) to make the banners that marked the racecourse snap (привязка ипподрома) and flutter now and then.   

In the silence of the broad green meadows one could hear the music winding throughout (музыкальная обмотка на протяжении всего) the city streets,(улицы города)  farther and nearer and ever approaching,(когда-либо приближающийся)  a cheerful faint sweetness (веселая слабая сладость) of the air from time to time trembled (время дрогнуло) and gathered together (собрались вместе) and broke out into the great joyous clanging (большой радостный лязг) of the bells.  

Joyous!

How is one to tell about joy?

How describe the citizens of Omelas?

They were not simple folk,(простой народный)  you see, though they were happy.

But we do not say the words of cheer much (подбадривать) any more.

All smiles have become archaic.(стать архаичным) 

Given a description such as this one tends (один стремится) to make certain assumptions.(делать определенные предположения)    

Given a description such as this one tends (один стремится) to look next (смотри дальше) for the King, mounted on a splendid stallion (великолепный жеребец) and surrounded by his noble knights,(благородные рыцари)  or perhaps in a golden litter borne (золотой помет) by great-muscled slaves.(мускулистые рабы) 

But there was no king.

They did not use swords,(использовать мечи)  or keep slaves.(держать рабов) 

They were not barbarians, I do not know the rules and laws of their society, but I suspect that they were singularly few.   

As they did without monarchy (без монархии) and slavery, so they also got (также получил) on without the stock exchange,(фондовая биржа)   the advertisement, the secret police,(Тайная полиция)  and the bomb.

Yet I repeat that these were not simple folk,(простой народный)   not dulcet shepherds,(сладкие пастухи)  noble savages,(благородные дикари)  bland utopians.(пресные утописты) 

There were not less complex (менее сложный) than us.  

The trouble is that we have a bad habit,(плохая привычка)  encouraged by pedants and sophisticates, of considering happiness (учитывая счастье) as something rather stupid.(что-то довольно глупое) 

Only pain is intellectual, only evil interesting.(зло интересно) 

This is the treason of the artist: a refusal to admit the banality of evil and the terrible boredom (ужасная скука) of pain.

If you can't lick 'em, join 'em.

If it hurts, repeat it.

  

But to praise despair (хвалить отчаяние) is to condemn delight,(осуждать наслаждение)  to embrace violence (принимать насилие) is to lose hold (терять хватку) of everything else.(все остальное)   

We have almost lost hold;(почти потерял хватку)  we can no longer describe happy man,(больше описывать счастливого человека)  nor make any celebration of joy.

How can I tell you about the people of Omelas?

They were not naive and happy children--though their children were, in fact, happy.

They were mature, intelligent, passionate adults whose lives (страстные взрослые, чья жизнь) were not wretched.

O miracle!

But I wish I could describe (мог бы описать) it better.   

I wish I could convince (мог убедить) you.

Omelas sounds (звуки омелас) in my words like (такие слова, как) a city in a fairy tale,(сказка)   long ago (давно) and far away,(далеко)  once upon a time.

Perhaps it would be best if you imagined it as your own  fancy bids,(причудливые ставки)  assuming it will rise to the occasion,for certainly I cannot suit (не подходит) you all.  

For instance, how about technology?

I think that there would be no cars or helicopters in and above the streets;  this follows from the fact that the people of Omelas are happy people.(счастливые люди)    

Happiness is based on a just discrimination of what is necessary, what is neither necessary (нет необходимости) nor destructive, and what is destructive.

In the middle category,(средняя категория)  however--that of the unnecessary but undestructive, that of comfort, luxury, exuberance, etc.-they could  perfectly well have central heating,(центральное отопление)  subway trains,(поезда метро)  washing machines,(стиральные машины)  and all kinds of marvelous devices (чудесные устройства) not yet invented (еще не изобретено) here, floating light-(плавающий свет) sources, fuelless power,(бестопливная мощность)  a cure for the common cold.(простуда) 

Or they could have none of that: it doesn't matter.   

As you like it.

I incline to think that people from towns up and down the coast have been coming  to to Omelas during the last days (Последние дни) before the Festival on very fast little trains (быстрые маленькие поезда) and double-decked trams,(палубные трамваи)   and that the trains station (железнодорожная станция) of Omelas is actually the handsomest building (самое красивое здание) in town, though plainer (хотя проще) than the magnificent Farmers'(великолепные фермеры)  Market.   

But even granted trains,(даже предоставленные поезда)  I fear that Omelas so far strikes (дальние удары) some of you as goody-goody.  

Smiles, bells, parades, horses, bleh.

If so, please add (пожалуйста, добавьте) an orgy.

If an orgy would help,(оргия поможет)  don't hesitate.

Let us (Давайте) not, however, have temples from which issue beautiful nude priests (выдает красивых обнаженных попов) and priestesses already half (жрицы уже наполовину) in ecstasy and ready to copulate with any man or woman, lover or stranger, who desires union (желает союза) with the deep godhead (глубокое божество) of the blood, although that was my first idea.(первая идея)    

But really it would be better not to have any temples in Omelas--at least, not manned temples.(храмы с людьми) 

Religion

yes, clergy no.

Surely the beautiful nudes (красивые обнаженные тела) can just wander about, offering themselves like divine souffles (как божественное суфле) to the hunger of the needy and the rapture of the flesh.

Let them join the processions.   

Let tambourines (пусть бубны) be struck above the copulations, and the gory of desire be proclaimed upon (провозглашенный) the gongs, and (a not unimportant point)(неважный момент)  let the offspring of these delightful rituals (восхитительные ритуалы) be beloved and looked after by all.

One thing (одна вещь) I know there is none of in Omelas is guilt.

But what else should there be?   

I thought at first there were no drugs, but that is puritanical.

For those who like it, the faint  insistent sweetness of drooz may perfume (drooz майские духи) the ways of the city, drooz which first brings (сначала приносит) a great  lightness and brilliance to the mind and limbs, and then after some hours a dreamy languor,(мечтательное томление)  and wonderful visions (чудесные видения) at last of the very arcane and inmost secrets (сокровенные секреты) of the Universe, as well as exciting the pleasure of sex beyond (секс за гранью) all belief; and it is not habit-forming.

For more modest tastes (скромные вкусы) I think there ought to be beer.

What else, what else belongs (остальное принадлежит) in the joyous city?(радостный город)    

The sense of victory, surely, the celebration of courage.

But as we did without clergy,(без духовенства)  let us (Давайте) do without soldiers.(без солдат) 

The joy built upon successful slaughter (радость, построенная на успешном забое) is not the right kind (правильный вид) of joy; it will not do; it is fearful and it is trivial.   

A boundless and generous contentment,(щедрое довольство)  a magnanimous triumph felt (великодушный триумф) not against some outer enemy (внешний враг) but in communion with the finest and fairest in the souls of all men everywhere (мужчины везде) and the splendor of the world's summer:

This is what swells the hearts of the people of Omelas, and the victory they celebrate is that of life.

I don't think many (думаю многие) of them need to take drooz.(взять дроз)   

Most of the processions have reached the Green Fields (зеленые поля) by now.

A marvelous smell (чудесный запах) of cooking goes forth (готовка идет вперед) from the red and blue tents (синие палатки) of the provisioners.

The faces of small children (маленькие дети) are amiably sticky;(дружелюбно липкий)  in the benign gray beard (доброкачественная седая борода) of a man a couple of crumbs of rich pastry (богатая выпечка) are entangled.

The youths and girls have mounted their horses and are beginning to group around (группироваться вокруг) the starting line (Стартовая линия) of the course.

An old woman,(старая женщина)  small, fat, and laughing, is passing out flowers from a basket, and tall young men wear (высокие молодые люди носят) her flowers in their shining hair.(блестящие волосы)    

A child of nine or ten sits (десять мест) at the edge of the crowd alone,(толпа одна)  playing on a wooden flute.(деревянная флейта) 

People pause (люди делают паузу) to listen, and they smile, but they do not speak to him, for he never ceases playing (никогда не перестает играть) and never sees (никогда не видит) them, his dark eyes wholly rapt (темные глаза полностью увлечены) in the sweet, thing magic (магия вещей) of the tune.

He finishes, and slowly lowers (медленно опускается) his hands holding (руки держат) the wooden flute.(деревянная флейта)   

As if that little private silence (немного личного молчания) were the signal, all at once a trumpet sounds (звуки трубы) from the pavilion near (павильон возле) the starting line:(Стартовая линия)  imperious, melancholy, piercing.

The horses rear (лошади сзади) on their slender legs,(стройные ноги)  and some of them neigh in answer.   

Sober-faced, the young riders stroke (инсульт молодых гонщиков) the horses' necks and soothe them, whispering.

"Quiet, quiet, there my beauty, my hope..."

They begin to form in rank along (ранжировать) the starting line.(Стартовая линия) 

The crowds along (толпы вдоль) the racecourse are like a field of grass and flowers in the wind.

The Festival of Summer has begun.  

Do you believe?

Do you accept the festival, the city, the joy?

No?

Then let me describe one (описать один) more thing.

In a basement under one of the beautiful public buildings (красивые общественные здания) of Omelas, or perhaps in the cellar of one of its spacious private homes,(просторные частные дома)  there is a room.

It has one locked door,(одна запертая дверь)  and no window.   

A little light seeps (немного света просачивается) in dustily between cracks in the boards, secondhand from a cobwebbed window somewhere across (окно где-то затянуто паутиной) the cellar.

In one corner (один угол) of the little room (маленькая комната) a couple of mops, with stiff, clotted, foul-smelling heads,(нюхающие головы)  stand near (стоять рядом) a rusty bucket.(ржавое ведро) 

The floor is dirt, a little damp (немного сыро) to the touch, as cellar dirt usually (подвальная грязь обычно) is.  

The room is about three paces long (три шага в длину) and two wide:(два широких)  a mere broom closet (простой шкаф для метел) or disused tool room.(заброшенная инструментальная комната) 

In the room, a child is sitting.

It could be a boy or a girl.

It looks about six, but actually is nearly ten.(почти десять) 

It is feeble-minded.

Perhaps it was born defective,(родился неполноценным)  or perhaps it has become imbecile (стать слабоумным) through fear, malnutrition, and neglect.

It picks its nose and occasionally fumbles vaguely (иногда невнятно шарит) with its toes or genitals, as it sits hunched (сидит сгорбившись) in the corner farthest (самый дальний угол) from the bucket and the two mops.(две швабры)    

It is afraid of the mops.

It finds them horrible.

It shuts its eyes, but it knows the mops are still standing (все еще стоял) there; and the door is locked; and nobody will come.

The door is always locked;(всегда заперт)  and nobody ever comes,(никто никогда не приходит)  except that sometimes--the child has no understanding of time or interval--sometimes the door rattles terribly (дверь дребезжит ужасно) and opens, and a person, or several people,(несколько человек)  are there.

One of them may come (Может прийти) in and kick the child to make it stand up.

The others never come close,(другие никогда не приближаются)  but peer in at it with frightened, disgusted eyes.(глаза отвращения)    

The food bowl (миска для еды) and the water jug (кувшин для воды) are hastily filled,(наспех заполненный)  the door is locked; the eyes disappear.(глаза исчезают)   

The people at the door never say anything,(дверь никогда ничего не говори)  but the child, who has not always lived (всегда жил) in the tool room,(мастерская)  and can remember sunlight (помни солнечный свет) and its mother's voice, sometimes speaks.(иногда говорит) 

"I will be good, " it says.

"Please let (пожалуйста, давайте) me out.

I will be good!"

They never answer.(никогда не отвечай) 

The child used (ребенок использовал) to scream for help at night, and cry a good deal,(хорошая сделка)  but now it only makes a kind of whining, "eh-haa, eh-haa," and it speaks less (меньше говорит) and less often.(менее часто)   

It is so thin there are no calves to its legs; its belly protrudes;(живот выпирает)  it lives on a half-bowl of corn meal (кукурузная мука) and grease a day.

It is naked.

Its buttocks and thighs are a mass of festered sores,(гноящиеся язвы)  as it sits in its own excrement continually.(экскременты постоянно)   

They all know it is there, all the people of Omelas.

Some of them have come to see it, others are content merely (содержание просто) to know it is there.

They all know that it has to be there.

Some of them understand why, and some do not, but they all understand that their happiness, the beauty of their city, the tenderness of their friendships, the health of their children, the wisdom of their scholars, the skill of their makers, even the abundance of their harvest and the kindly weathers (добрая погода) of their skies, depend wholly (полностью зависеть) on this child's abominable misery.(отвратительное страдание)   

This is usually explained (обычно объясняется) to children when they are between eight and twelve, whenever they seem capable (казаться способным) of understanding; and most of those who come to see the child are young people,(молодежь)  though often enough (хотя достаточно часто) an adult comes,(взрослый приходит)  or comes back,(возвращается)  to see the child.

No matter how well the matter has been explained to them, these young spectators (юные зрители) are always shocked (всегда в шоке) and sickened at the sight.

They feel disgust,(чувствовать отвращение)  which they had thought themselves superior to.   

They feel anger,(чувствовать гнев)  outrage, impotence, despite all the explanations.

They would like (хотел бы) to do something for the child.

But there is nothing they can do.

If the child were brought up into the sunlight out of that vile place,(мерзкое место)  if it were cleaned and fed and comforted, that would be a good thing,(хорошая вещь)  indeed; but if it were done, in that day and hour all the prosperity and beauty and delight of Omelas would wither (омелас засохнет) and be destroyed.

Those are the terms.   

To exchange all the goodness and grace of every life (каждая жизнь) in Omelas for that single, small improvement:(небольшое улучшение)  to throw away (выбросить) the happiness of thousands for the chance of happiness of one: that would be to let guilt within (пусть вина внутри) the walls indeed.(стены действительно) 

The terms are strict and absolute; there may not even be a kind word spoken (доброе слово сказанное) to the child.

Often the young people go home (молодые люди идут домой) in tears, or in a tearless rage,(бесслезная ярость)  when they have seen the child and faced this terrible paradox.(ужасный парадокс)    

They may brood (может размышлять) over it for weeks or years.

But as time goes (время идет) on they begin to realize that even if the child could (ребенок мог) be released,  it would not get much good (получить много хорошего) of its freedom: a little vague pleasure (маленькое смутное удовольствие) of warmth and food, no real doubt,(настоящее сомнение)  but little more.  

It is too degraded and imbecile to know any real joy.(настоящая радость) 

It has been afraid too long ever (давно) to be free of fear.  

Its habits are too uncouth for it to respond to humane treatment.(гуманное обращение) 

Indeed, after so long it would probably (вероятно,) be wretched without walls (убогий без стен) about it to protect it, and darkness for its eyes, and its own excrement to sit in.  

Their tears at the bitter injustice dry (горькая несправедливость сухая) when they begin to perceive the terrible justice (ужасное правосудие) of reality, and to accept it.

Yet it is their tears and anger, the trying of their generosity and the acceptance of their helplessness, which are perhaps the true source (истинный источник) of the splendor of their lives.

Theirs is no vapid, irresponsible happiness.(безответственное счастье) 

They know that they, like the child, are not free.

They know compassion.(знать сострадание)    

It is the existence of the child, and their knowledge of its existence, that makes possible (делает возможным) the nobility of their architecture, the poignancy of their music, the profundity of their science.

It is because of the child that they are so gentle with children.   

They know that if the wretched one (несчастный) were not there sniveling in the dark, the other one, the flute-player, could make (мог бы сделать) no joyful music (радостная музыка) as the young riders line (линия юных райдеров) up in their beauty for the race in the sunlight of the first morning (первое утро) of summer.  

Now do you believe them?

Are they not more credible?

But there is one more thing to tell, and this is quite incredible.(совершенно невероятно) 

At times one (раз один) of the adolescent girls (девочки-подростки) or boys who go see (пойти посмотреть) the child does not go home (иди домой) (иди домой) to weep or rage, does not, in fact, go home at all.  

Sometimes also (иногда также) a man or a woman much older falls silent (женщина намного старше замолкает) for a day or two, then leaves home.(уходит из дома) 

These people go (люди идут) out into the street, and walk down the street alone.(улица одна) 

They keep walking,(Продолжай идти)  and walk straight (идти прямо) out of the city of Omelas, through the beautiful gates.(красивые ворота) 

They keep walking across (продолжай идти) the farmlands of Omelas.

Each one goes alone,(один идет один)  youth or girl, man or woman.  

Night falls;(наступает ночь)  the traveler must pass (путешественник должен пройти) down village streets,(деревенские улицы)  between the houses with yellow- lit windows,(освещенные окна)  and on out into the darkness of the fields.

Each alone, they go west (идти на запад) or north, towards the mountains.

They go on.

They leave Omelas,(оставь омелас)  they walk ahead (идти вперед) into the darkness, and they do not come back.(Вернись)    

The place they go towards (перейти к) is a place even less imaginable (место еще менее вообразимое) to most of us than the city of happiness.

I cannot describe (не могу описать) it at all.

It is possible that it does not exist.

But they seem to know where they are going, the ones who walk away (уходи) from Omelas.
